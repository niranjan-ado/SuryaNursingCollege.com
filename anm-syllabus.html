Key Guidelines & Rules

  • Passing Marks: A candidate must secure 50% in aggregate for each subject, passing in theory and practical exams separately.
  • Attendance: A minimum of 80% attendance in theory and practicals is required to be eligible for examinations. 100% attendance in practical areas is required for the award of the diploma.
  • Attempts: A maximum of 3 attempts, including the first, are permitted for each paper.
  • Promotion: A candidate failing in more than one subject will not be promoted to the next year.
  • Internship: The principal must certify the successful completion of the internship program and 100% of clinical requirements before the award of the diploma.

Detailed Subject Breakdown

This course introduces the concepts of community health, primary healthcare, and the role of health teams. Students learn to conduct home visits, assess community health needs, and communicate effectively for health promotion.

Focuses on nutrition, hygiene, and mental health. This includes planning balanced diets, understanding the human body, promoting environmental sanitation, and identifying mental health needs in the community.

Covers the prevention and restoration of health. Key topics include infection control, immunization, management of common communicable diseases, primary medical care, and first aid for emergencies.

This course focuses on the assessment of growth and development in children, their nutritional needs, care during common illnesses, school health programs, and the rights of children.

A comprehensive course covering the human reproductive system, normal pregnancy, labor, and postnatal care. Students learn to conduct normal deliveries, identify high-risk pregnancies, and manage obstetric emergencies.

Prepares students to organize and manage a sub-center, maintain stock and supplies, implement national health programs, and coordinate with other health team members and agencies.

Clinical Experience & Competencies

Practical experience is a cornerstone of the ANM program, ensuring students are competent and confident in real-world healthcare settings.

Community Health Nursing and Health Promotion

Expected Competency Hospital Experience (30 hrs) Community Experience (280 hrs)
Community assessment and home visiting. Health assessment of (five) persons. Village mapping, assessing health concepts of five families.
Perform nutritional assessment. Assess two patients with malnutrition. Group work on nutrition exhibition and cooking demonstrations.
Assess environmental sanitation. - Assess a community's sanitation, disinfect one well, prepare reports.

Child Health Nursing

Expected Competency Hospital Experience (80 hrs) Community Experience (100 hrs)
Assess growth and development of children. Assess 10 children of different ages and record on chart. Conduct a school health clinic, assess growth, and refer.
Care of the sick child. Give care to 5 children as per IMNCI protocol. Give care to 5 children with diarrhoea and ARI at home.
Counsel mothers about feeding. Counsel mothers on breastfeeding. Demonstrate complementary food preparation.

Midwifery

Expected Competency Hospital Experience (220 hrs) Community Experience (160 hrs)
Assessment and care of normal pregnant women. Examine 20 antenatal women. Administer TT injection to 10 women. Conduct antenatal examinations at home. Counsel on birth preparedness.
Conducting normal delivery. Conduct 10 safe deliveries, plot partographs, assist in suturing. Conduct deliveries in health centers/home.
Counsel eligible couples on contraception. Prepare acceptors for sterilization, insert IUCDs. Distribute oral pills and condoms.
