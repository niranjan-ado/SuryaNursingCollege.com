GNM Syllabus

Official Diploma in General Nursing & Midwifery Curriculum (Revised 2015) by the Indian Nursing Council.

Program Philosophy & Aims

The Diploma in General Nursing and Midwifery is geared to meet the health needs of individuals, families, and the community. It is built on the belief that health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being. The program aims to prepare nurses with a sound educational foundation to function as efficient members of the health team in all kinds of healthcare settings.

Aims of the Program

  • To prepare nurses with competencies for first-level positions in all healthcare settings.
  • To develop an ability to cooperate and coordinate with members of the health team.
  • To foster personal and professional development to make a maximum contribution to society.
  • To serve as a base for further professional education and specialization.
  • To prepare nurses to keep pace with the latest professional and technological developments.

Objectives

Upon completion, the nurse will be able to demonstrate competency in providing healthcare using the nursing process, apply knowledge from humanities and sciences, function effectively in a team, mobilize community resources, and demonstrate interest in continuing education for professional development.

Course Structure (3 Years)

The GNM program is a three-year course with an integrated internship. The curriculum is divided into theoretical instruction and practical clinical experience to ensure comprehensive learning.

Scheme of Examination

Assessment includes internal assessments by the school and final examinations conducted by the State Nursing Council/Board. The minimum pass mark is 50% for each theory and practical paper separately (40% for English).

Key Examination Guidelines

  • A candidate can take any number of attempts within a maximum period of 6 years.
  • All previous papers must be cleared before appearing in the final year examination.
  • If a candidate fails in either theory or practical, they must re-appear for both papers.
  • Practical exams are conducted jointly by one internal and one external examiner in the respective clinical areas.

Admission & Regulations

Admission Terms and Conditions

  • Minimum Education: 10+2 with English, having obtained a minimum of 40% marks. Candidates from State Open School (recognized by State Govt.) and NIOS (recognized by Central Govt.) are also eligible. Science is preferable.
  • Alternate Qualification: 10+2 with English having 40% marks in Vocational ANM course or Vocational Stream-Health Care Science. Registered ANMs are also eligible.
  • Age: Minimum 17 years as of December 31st of the admission year. The upper age limit is 35 years.
  • Medical Fitness: Candidates must be medically fit.

Reservation Policy

A 5% relaxation in total marks is provided for SC/ST candidates. A 3% disability reservation is considered for candidates with 40-50% locomotor disability of the lower extremity. Other reservations are as per State Government policy.

Physical & Clinical Facilities

The Indian Nursing Council mandates specific requirements for establishing a School of Nursing, including a separate teaching block, a 100-bedded parent hospital, and adequate hostel facilities. This ensures a high-quality learning environment with sufficient clinical exposure for all students.
